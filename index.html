    • About Me

    Playing video games most of my life, I would always wonder about the processes they go through to become the thing we see and play. I have also always had a deep drive to help educate people, probably stemming from my drive to self-teach from a young age.

    So, in the summer of 2019 I decided I was going to learn about development, going so far as to change from my BSc Real Estate course to BSc Games Technology. It was scary at the time but honestly I wasn't very happy before switching courses so it was for the best. Now I'm able to do passion projects that mean a great deal to me and actually call it work, with it all going into my portfolio, how great is that?!

    To avoid delving into too much pointless detail, I'll just say that for years I couldn't find the right career fit. I have never been too interested in academic work, while always loving practical work. I've explored so many different career paths but I was either medically unable to do them or they just weren't as interesting as I'd hoped. Being a programmer, while highly frustrating at times, I believe is the perfect career path and I haven't looked back since.
